2. CONSENT CALENDAR  An item may be placed on the consent calendar by the Commission Secretary when an item is expected to be routinely approved without discussion or debate. The consent calendar shall be voted upon as one item. Commissioners or members of the public may request that an individual item be removed from the consent calendar, in which case the item shall be considered after the vote on the remainder of the consent calendar. Commission Members who do not wish to discuss an item on the consent calendar but who wish to vote no or to abstain, may direct the Commission Secretary to record a no vote or abstention for that item.

a. Project: 2670 & 2680 Walnut Boulevard Subdivision  Major Subdivision, requested waivers pursuant to  State Density Bonus law, Design Review, Tree Removal Permit,  & Tree Dripline Encroachment Permit  Application No. Y21-024; Addresses: 2670 & 2680 Walnut Boulevard APNs 179-030-002 & 179-030-001
 
Description: A proposed 13-lot, single family development at 2670 and 2680 Walnut Boulevard.  The project requires a major subdivision for the 13 lots, Design Review for 13 new single family homes, tree removal permits for the removal of 23 trees (10 of which are considered highly protected), and a tree dripline encroachment permit to allow the construction within the dripline of existing trees on site to be preserved.  Two of the 13 total units will be restricted to low-income households (50%- 80% AMI).  The project is seeking several waivers under State Density Bonus Law, as well as processing pursuant to the "The Housing Crisis Act of 2019 (Senate Bill 330).
 
The Planning Commission is being asked to approve the Major Subdivision (vesting tentative map), requested waivers pursuant to State Density Bonus, Tree Removal Permit, and Tree Dripline Encroachment Permit.  If approved, the project would then return to the Design Review Commission for final Design Review approval.

b. Project: Citrus Marketplace Shopping Center Rezone Application No. Y21-117; Addresses: 2200 - 2290 Oak Grove Road APNs 134-460-013, 134-460-014 & 134-460-015
 
Description: Rezone the Citrus Marketplace Shopping Center from Planned Development (P-D) District no. 1936 to a new P-D District that would allow a change in the operating hours for the vacant anchor tenant (former Nob Hill), and a change in the hours for outgoing deliveries from the BevMo store.  The rezoning would also update the shopping center’s land use regulations to more closely match those that apply to the other neighborhood shopping centers located in Walnut Creek. There are no changes proposed to the hours for incoming deliveries, and no exterior modifications or expansion of the shopping center is proposed as part of this application.
 
The Planning Commission is being asked to forward a recommendation to the City Council regarding adoption of a draft Negative Declaration and the new Planned Development Ordinance.
