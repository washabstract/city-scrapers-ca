5. PUBLIC HEARING

a. CONDUCT PUBLIC HEARING AND ADOPT AN ORDINANCE OF THE CITY COUNCIL OF THE CITY OF WALNUT CREEK ADDING CHAPTER 8 (SINGLE-USE FOODWARE ACCESSORIES AND CONDIMENTS) TO TITLE 5 (SANITATION AND HEALTH) OF THE WALNUT CREEK MUNICIPAL CODE ADOPTING BY REFERENCE CHAPTER 5.2 (COMMENCING WITH SECTION 42270) OF PART 3 OF DIVISION 30 OF THE PUBLIC RESOURCES CODE PROHIBITING A FOOD FACILITY FROM PROVIDING ANY SINGLE-USE FOODWARE ACCESSORY OR STANDARD CONDIMENT UNLESS REQUESTED BY CONSUMER AND AUTHORIZING CITY AND COUNTY ENFORCEMENT AND PENALTIES

Administrative Analyst II Emlyn Struthers presented the agenda report. She reported that on October 5, 2021, Governor Newsom signed AB 1276 (Carillo) into law to prohibit a food facility from providing any single-use foodware accessory or standard condiment to a consumer unless requested by the consumer. AB 1276, requires cities to authorize an enforcement agency to enforce the law. Administrative Analyst II Struthers reported that in order to comply with the new law, the City may adopt the law by reference, naming the City and County as the enforcement agency.

Mayor Francois opened the item for public comment, hearing no comment, he closed the public comment period.

Motion by Darling, second by Wilk, carried unanimously, to WAIVE READING AND ADOPT ORDINANCE NO. 2218 adding Chapter 8 (Single-Use Foodware Accessories and Condiments) to Title 5 (Sanitation and Health) of the Walnut Creek Municipal Code to adopt by reference Chapter 5.2 (commencing with Section 42270) of Part 3 of Division 30 of the California Public Resources Code prohibiting a food facility from providing any single-use foodware accessory or standard condiment unless requested by consumer and authorizing City and County enforcement and penalties.

YEAS: Darling, Haskew, Wilk, Mayor Pro Tem Silva, Mayor Francois.

d. CONSIDERATION OF URGENCY ORDINANCE PERTAINING TO CONTINUATION OF THE REBOUND PROGRAM

Senior Planner Jeanine Cavalli presented the agenda report. She reported that in response to the COVID-19 pandemic, the City established the Rebound Program on May 21, 2020 in order to assist businesses desiring to operate outdoors. While the Rebound Program may remain in place throughout the entirety of the local Emergency Proclamation, the City has implemented the program in intervals of approximately 6 months since its inception. Senior Planner Cavalli stated that while the City works on the adoption of a Permanent Outdoor Dining Program, the City may choose to continue to allow temporary outdoor dining and retail display by continuing the Rebound Program through June 2023 through the adoption of an urgency ordinance.

Mayor Francois opened the item for public comment, hearing no comment, he closed the public comment period. Written public comments were received and provided to the City Council, and posted online as part of the meeting record.

Motion by Wilk, second by Haskew, carried unanimously, to WAIVE READING AND ADOPT URGENCY ORDINANCE NO. 2219 continuing the Rebound Program, temporarily suspending and adding certain requirements and authorities under the Walnut Creek Municipal Code, including requirements pertaining to parking, signs, and curbside pickup zones, and temporarily vesting certain authorities in the City Manager to allow for implementing an administrative citation and fine structure to enforce Rebound Program Guidelines.

YEAS: Darling, Haskew, Wilk, Mayor Pro Tem Silva, Mayor Francois.
